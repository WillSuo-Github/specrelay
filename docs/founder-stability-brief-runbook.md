# Founder Stability Brief Runbook

Date: 2026-05-16

Status: staged internal runbook. Use this only if the current contractor/agency wedge downgrades at the hard checkpoint in `docs/post-three-touch-decision-tree.md` or a founder-side signal arrives first.

## Purpose

This runbook turns the founder-side pivot into one exact validation motion instead of another abstract repositioning note.

The product hypothesis becomes:

```text
Before you launch or hand an AI-built app to someone else, freeze the basic flows, log the blockers you actually hit, and make one clear launch-or-fix decision.
```

## Trigger

Activate this runbook only when one of these becomes true:

1. Afterbuild, Bytewise, and AppStuck are all silent or negative at `2026-05-21 23:59 CST`.
2. The contractor/agency batch produces only weak positives and Product/CEO decides the buyer is still too indirect.
3. A founder or builder explicitly says the smaller stability brief sounds more realistic than a full handoff packet.

## Exact Validation Ask

Use this question as the anchor:

```text
Before you launch or ask someone else to fix your AI-built app, would you fill out a short stability brief that locks the core flows, blockers, risky paths, and a launch decision?
```

The useful answer is not “cool idea.”

The useful answer is one of:

- “Yes, I would do that before launch.”
- “Yes, I would do that before handing it to a contractor.”
- “No, I would still just click around and start fixing things.”
- “Only if it takes under X minutes.”

## Primary Output Shape

If this pivot activates, the product output should be treated as five sections:

1. Core flows that must keep working
2. Observed blockers from real use
3. Stability risks and affected paths
4. Launch decision: ship / fix-first / hold
5. Optional next-builder notes

## Best-Fit Evaluators

| Segment | Why useful | Filter |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hands-on founder using Lovable/Bolt/Replit/Cursor/v0 | closest user for a self-serve brief | actively launching or using an internal workflow app |
| Operator running a fragile internal AI-built tool | feels stability pain before formal product work | app is live inside a team, not just a demo |
| Founder about to hire a contractor for the first cleanup pass | still close to handoff, but buyer is the founder | no repo audit flow yet |

## Staged No-Link Community Reply

Use only where a text-first reply is safer than a product link:

```text
For an AI-built app, I think the useful pre-launch artifact is smaller than a full spec:

1. lock the 3-5 flows that must keep working
2. note the blockers you actually hit while using it
3. mark the risky paths those blockers touch
4. decide ship now, fix first, or hold
5. give the next person that brief instead of vague requirements

That feels more realistic to me than pretending the first pass is a full handoff doc.
```

Gate: public reply remains action-time gated.

## Staged Direct Ask

Use only after a founder-side target is selected and approval exists for the route:

```text
I am testing a smaller pre-launch artifact for AI-built apps.

Instead of a full handoff packet, it captures:
- the core flows that must keep working
- the blockers found from real use
- the risky paths
- one launch-or-fix decision

The useful feedback is simple: would you actually fill this out before launch or before handing the app to someone else?
```

Gate: direct outreach remains action-time gated.

## Signal Criteria

Strong positive:

- the founder says they would complete the brief before launch or before asking for help
- they give a time budget such as “yes if under 5 minutes”
- they say one missing section would make it usable

Weak positive:

- they agree the workflow feels realistic but do not commit to using it
- they prefer the smaller brief over the full packet, but still without usage intent

Negative:

- they would never document anything before launch/help
- they still want repo-connected automation first
- they say they would only trust live app exploration and no artifact

## Immediate Product Move If Activated

1. Rewrite the first-screen promise around launch-or-fix clarity.
2. Reduce visible output to the five sections above.
3. Downplay agency-intake language.
4. Keep the current static/no-login constraint.
