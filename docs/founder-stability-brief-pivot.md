# Founder-Side Stability Brief Pivot

Date: 2026-05-15

Status: staged internal pivot asset. Use this only if the current contractor/agency wedge downgrades to weak-positive-only or silent/negative at the hard checkpoint in `docs/post-three-touch-decision-tree.md`.

## Why This Exists

SpecRelay's strongest real language is still not "I need a contractor intake packet."

The clearest useful signal is narrower:

- Jesse described a stability-first workflow where he plays with the app, promotes improvements from observed issues, and starts with basics before adding features.
- Frank described a simple founder-maintained app that does not hand off early.
- Three contractor/agency touches are now in market, but none has yet proven that a founder-filled packet changes intake behavior.

If the contractor/agency wedge remains weak, the company should not keep pretending the intake buyer is validated. The safer adjacent wedge is a founder-side stability brief used before launch, before asking for help, or before another builder touches the app.

## New Product Claim

```text
Before you ask a contractor to fix or improve an AI-built app, freeze the basic flows, list the observed blockers, mark the risky paths, and make one launch decision.
```

This is not a full handoff packet. It is a smaller pre-review brief.

## Who This Is For

Primary user:

- founder or operator who built an internal tool, workflow app, or small SaaS prototype with Lovable, Bolt, Replit, Cursor, or v0

Good-fit situations:

- the founder still owns the app day to day
- the app is close to a pilot, internal rollout, or first paying users
- the next task is deciding what must be stabilized before sharing it wider

Bad-fit situations:

- agency intake teams that already require repo/app access before quoting
- founders who only want automated repo scanning
- teams already running a mature QA and release process

## Minimum Useful Output

If the pivot is activated, the output should shrink to five sections:

1. Core flows that must keep working
2. Observed blockers from real use
3. Stability risks and affected paths
4. Launch decision: safe to pilot, fix first, or hold
5. Optional reviewer notes for the next builder

Remove or downplay:

- readiness scoring as the headline
- broad engineering-brief language aimed at agencies
- anything that implies full contractor intake completeness

## Positioning Shift

Old framing:

- handoff packet between a vibe-coded prototype and a serious launch review

Pivot framing:

- stability-first launch brief for AI-built apps before wider rollout or outside help

Suggested homepage promise:

```text
Freeze the basic flows in your AI-built app before launch, review, or handoff.
```

Suggested subtext:

```text
Capture what works, what breaks, and what must be fixed first so the next person does not start from guesswork.
```

## Validation Questions

Use these when the pivot becomes active:

1. Before asking for help on an AI-built app, do you first need a clear list of working flows and known blockers?
2. Would a short stability brief save you time, or would you still just click around the app and start from scratch?
3. Which output matters most: launch decision, broken paths, risk list, or reviewer notes?
4. Would you create this before a pilot or only after something breaks?
5. Is this useful enough to pay for before launch or before handing the app to someone else?

## Strong Signal For The Pivot

Count as strong pivot evidence if a founder says any of these clearly:

- "I would use this before letting a contractor touch the app."
- "I need this before I launch or invite users."
- "This is smaller and more realistic than a full handoff packet."

Weak signal:

- agreement that stability matters without saying they would use the brief
- generic interest in QA or launch readiness

Negative signal:

- they still want repo-connected automation first
- they say they would not document anything before asking for help

## If Activated

Immediate changes:

1. Rewrite homepage headline and first-screen copy toward the stability brief.
2. Simplify exported output to the five-section version above.
3. Stop contractor/agency outreach until founder-side usage is tested.
4. Stage one founder-facing validation ask instead of another rescue-service ask.
